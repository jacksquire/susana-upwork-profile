02 Upwork best practices that shaped this rewrite

  • The first two lines are the whole game. Upwork shows only the opening of your overview in search results and previews. It must hook a skimming buyer with an outcome, not open with a résumé summary.
  • Specialize, don't list. Upwork's matching algorithm and buyer trust both reward a sharp niche. "I do five things" reads as generalist. Lead with one hero skill; let the rest support it.
  • Specific proof beats adjectives. "Evaluated 5,000+ AI medical responses" persuades; "high-quality work" does not. Numbers, domains, and volumes are what justify a hire.
  • Sell the outcome, not the task. Buyers don't want "translation," they want "medical meaning that survives the language change." Frame around what they get.
  • Make the next step obvious. End with a simple instruction on how to start and what to include in a message. Lower the friction to reach out.
  • Match the audience's register. Medical and AI-company buyers respond to calm credibility, not hype or heavy emoji. Confidence, precision, restraint.

03 Feedback on the current profile

The current profile is competent and clean. It reads like a careful CV, which is exactly the ceiling we want to break through.

What's working

  • Strong, rare credential: MD licensed in two countries.
  • Clear differentiator stated: clinical expertise + bilingual.
  • Names real tools (Heidi, ChatGPT, Claude) — signals she's AI-fluent.
  • Clean services list; no typos or fluff.
  • Excellent underlying stats (100% JSS, $50K+, 2,723 hrs).

What's holding it back

  • Opens with credentials, not a hook. The searchable preview is a CV summary, not a reason to keep reading.
  • The hero skill is buried. AI medical evaluation is listed third, when it's her most valuable and in-demand offer.
  • Zero specificity. No volumes, domains, or named results anywhere.
  • Underpriced and the copy reinforces it — reads "safe generalist," not "scarce specialist."
  • No clear call to action or instruction on how to start a conversation.
  • Slightly generic phrasing ("ensure accuracy, clarity, and reliability") that any applicant could write.
